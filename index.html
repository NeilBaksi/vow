Premium product concept

Vow
the luxury operating system for modern weddings

A US-based planning platform where couples and planners can discover venues, curate inspiration, manage budgets, coordinate guests, and run the entire celebration from one refined workspace.

Opportunity

The market is large, emotional, and still fragmented.

Positioning

What Vow would be

For couples
  • Beautiful planning workspace that combines inspiration, vendor discovery, guest logistics, and payment tracking.
  • Luxury feel without planner-only complexity, with elegant onboarding based on date, budget, city, and aesthetic.
  • A wedding website, RSVP flow, registry layer, and day-of timeline that all stay in sync.
For planners & vendors
  • Multi-client dashboard for planners, with role-based collaboration, approvals, and communications.
  • Curated vendor storefronts with premium imagery, transparent packages, availability, and lead qualification.
  • High-intent marketplace model rather than a generic directory flooded with undifferentiated listings.

Experience vision

The interface should feel more like luxury hospitality than project management.

The market leaders offer planning tools, websites, registry features, or broad vendor directories, but reviews note tradeoffs between marketplace breadth, design quality, and collaboration depth. Vow’s edge would be premium curation plus a calmer, more editorial interface.

Build plan

A practical roadmap to launch

Phase 1
MVP marketplace + planning core
Auth, onboarding, vendor discovery, shortlist, budget, checklist, and wedding dashboard.
Phase 2
Guest tools + website builder
RSVP flows, guest list, wedding website templates, and seating chart logic.
Phase 3
Planner mode + AI concierge
Multi-client workspaces, vendor messaging, personalized recommendations, and smart task assistance.
Recommended stack
Next.js for the web app, Supabase for auth/database/storage, Stripe for payments, Mapbox or Google Maps for geographic discovery, and Figma for the design system and mockups.
Design workflow
Brand direction → information architecture → Figma wireframes → high-fidelity product mockups → clickable prototype → production design system.
Mockup sources
Figma offers wedding website templates and community inspiration, while dedicated UI kits such as Bridalova and wedding planner app case studies can accelerate concept exploration.
